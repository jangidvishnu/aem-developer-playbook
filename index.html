Career Strategy

Where AEM jobs show up in India and how to pick companies.

In India, AEM roles show up at product companies, GCCs, and agencies / SIs — each is different in pace, ownership, and interviews. Use Companies to shortlist, then Resume & LinkedIn templates to prep your profile, then How to Apply for day-to-day steps. Build clear depth in the AEM areas you actually work with — such as Sites, Cloud Service, Dispatcher, Forms, or integrations — rather than presenting an overly broad skill profile you cannot demonstrate. Even when you're not switching jobs, stay ready: keep LinkedIn/Naukri current, periodically review interview questions or take an interview when you are genuinely open to exploring a role, and fix whatever gaps show up. This isn't a ranking — every company has trade-offs.

AEM engineer (individual contributor)

From building components to owning Dispatcher, Cloud Manager, and performance.

  1. Foundation — HTL, Core Components, basic Sling Models, author/publish setup.
  2. Platform depth — Dispatcher, caching, OSGi services, Cloud Manager pipelines.
  3. Senior scope — Headless fragments, GraphQL, performance tuning, helping other teams on technical calls.

Solution / platform architect

Plan big AEM rollouts across brands and connect AEM to Analytics, Target, and AEP.

  1. Integration patterns — AEM with Analytics, Target, AEP; identity and consent setup.
  2. Scale and MSM — MSM blueprints, content workflows, CI/CD and environment planning.
  3. Modern stack — Edge Delivery, Universal Editor, headless and personalization at the edge.

Resume & LinkedIn templates

Copy-paste patterns for resume, LinkedIn, Naukri, and outreach.

Present yourself as an AEM Developer with clear skills in Sites, Cloud Service, Dispatcher, and integrations. Fill in your real projects and numbers — don't make up impact. Use these as writing patterns, not ready-made experience claims. Replace every placeholder with work you actually performed and can explain in detail. Templates below are for LinkedIn, Naukri, outreach messages, and resume bullets.

Resume bullet — AEM delivery impact

resume

Delivered [feature/migration you actually worked on] on AEM as a Cloud Service for [brand/program], improving [metric you can explain, e.g. publish lead time / cache hit ratio / Core Web Vitals] by [your measured change] through [Dispatcher tuning / pipeline automation / component refactor you did].

Resume bullet — headless / GraphQL

resume

Implemented headless content APIs using AEM Content Fragments and persisted GraphQL queries, enabling [web/mobile] teams to [specific outcome you delivered] without duplicate content entry.

STAR story frame — production incident

interview

Situation: [outage/symptom]. Task: [your role]. Action: [triage steps, collaboration, fix]. Result: [restored SLA, post-mortem, preventive change].

LinkedIn summary pattern — AEM developer

branding

AEM Developer with hands-on experience in [Sites/Assets/Cloud Service] and [Dispatcher/headless/personalization]. [One sentence on a real project outcome you can explain — e.g. reliable releases, faster publish cycle, or integration you owned.] Background in [Java/frontend/platform] and [industry]. Open to AEM roles in [city/remote].

networking

I am exploring [company/sector] roles where Adobe Experience Manager is central to the stack. Open to a brief conversation if this aligns with roles you are hiring for in [team/location].

Prefer roles where AEM is core to the product, not a side CMS.

resume

Improved publish-side performance for [site/program you worked on] by tuning Dispatcher [filter/cache/invalidation] rules and measuring [cache hit ratio / TTFB / error rate you tracked], reducing [symptom you observed] without changing authoring workflows.

Apply workflow

Prepare your resume and profiles first. When applying, check that the role's expected joining timeline is reasonably compatible with your availability.

  1. Draft your resume with an AI assistant if useful: Share your real experience — companies, technologies, education, projects, and employment history. State the AEM role you are targeting and the type of companies you are interested in. List your actual skills clearly and ask for a role-focused draft using relevant ATS keywords. Review every bullet manually and remove anything you cannot explain or defend in an interview.
  2. Do not publish an AI-generated resume draft as-is. If you use Overleaf, start with a simple one-column template and move the reviewed content into it carefully. Check formatting, dates, links, section order, and every bullet before exporting the PDF. Do not add GitHub, HackerRank, or other profile links unless you actively use them.
  3. Upload the Overleaf PDF to an AI assistant and a few free ATS checker sites. Use ATS checker scores as a rough sanity check, not as a hiring benchmark. Review missing keywords, parsing issues, section structure, and role alignment before deciding what to change.
  4. Review LinkedIn and Naukri section by section so your role, dates, skills, notice period, and experience are consistent with your resume. An AI assistant can help review individual sections, but verify every suggestion before publishing it. Use clear role and skill terminology that matches the work you actually do and the roles you are targeting. Spend real time on both profiles — they are essential for India hiring. Use the same resume details on other job portals. Keep LWD (last working day), location, CTC, notice period, and availability accurate.
  5. Notice period — prioritise roles that match your availability: If you are serving a long notice period and a posting explicitly asks for an immediate or very early joiner, prioritise other roles unless the employer indicates flexibility. Applying to roles with a clearly incompatible joining timeline can lead to an early rejection and may not be the best use of your application effort.
  6. Notice period — where you can apply: During a long notice period, you can still apply where the posting or recruiter indicates flexibility on joining dates. Some large employers run longer hiring processes or accept candidates serving standard notice periods, but this varies by role and team. State your last working day clearly and check the expected joining timeline.
  7. As your last working day gets closer, increase application and outreach activity for roles seeking earlier joiners. Around the final month of notice can be a useful point to widen daily searches, but always follow the joining expectations in the posting or recruiter conversation. Use early notice time to prepare your resume, LinkedIn, Naukri, company shortlist, and AEM interview prep.

Prioritise roles whose joining timeline matches your availability. If a posting explicitly requires an immediate joiner and you cannot start soon, focus your effort on better-matched openings.

Outreach

Use referrals, HR posts, and connection requests — avoid blind apply when you can reach a person.

  1. LinkedIn posts: Search AEM (or your role keyword), filter type to Posts. First pass — wider time window; after that — last 1 day only. Look for HR or hiring posts for your role. Often an email is in the post — send your application there. This gives you a direct application channel when a recruiter or hiring contact has explicitly shared their contact details.
  2. Gmail cover-letter template: For LinkedIn or Naukri invites, keep a reusable Gmail template — short cover letter with skills, experience, LWD if applicable, contact details, and resume attached. Reuse for every invite-style apply. For roles seeking early joiners, time this outreach closer to your availability. If the employer accepts a longer notice period, you can contact them earlier and state your last working day clearly.
  3. Referral before direct apply: When you see a job on LinkedIn or Naukri, do not apply immediately. Search that company on LinkedIn, find people in the same role (or a related AEM role), and send a connection request. After they accept, ask for a referral politely — not bluntly ("could you refer me?"). Use an AI-drafted message that mentions your role, experience, and why you fit. Wait for their reply. If the person is comfortable referring you, follow their process. If you do not receive a response, continue with the official application route rather than waiting indefinitely.
  4. Recruiter direct: If the post lists a recruiter email or contact info, reaching out there can be another route alongside a portal apply.
  5. Staffing / HR agencies: If a staffing agency is the stated hiring channel for a role, follow that route and verify the recruiter and employer details before sharing personal documents. You can also check whether the same opening appears on the employer's official careers site.

Use targeted outreach alongside official applications when you can identify a relevant recruiter, hiring contact, or potential referrer. Keep templates in Gmail; refine referral and HR-post wording with an AI assistant once, then reuse.

Train your LinkedIn feed

Shape LinkedIn so AEM jobs and hiring posts show up near the top when you open the app.

  1. Join AEM-focused LinkedIn groups (see Community links on this page) and turn on notifications for the most active ones.
  2. Follow AEM recruiters, hiring managers, and delivery leads who regularly post openings. Connect with people already in AEM roles at your target companies.
  3. Follow and engage with relevant AEM recruiters, engineers, groups, and hiring posts so your LinkedIn activity stays focused on the roles and topics you care about.
  4. Search AEM (Posts + Jobs) daily with a last-1-day filter once your notice window is open. Save useful searches and repeat them regularly instead of relying only on the home feed to surface relevant openings.

A focused network and repeatable search routine can reduce the time spent finding relevant AEM hiring posts.

Careers sites vs job boards

AEM openings can appear across employer careers sites, LinkedIn, Naukri, recruiter posts, and other job boards. The same opening may not be equally visible on every channel.

  1. Do not rely only on the Careers button. Recruiters often message you on Naukri or LinkedIn, or post on Foundit, Cutshort, and similar boards.
  2. In Companies, open each employer's detail panel and check AEM evidence and Hiring evidence links — those often point to board posts or searches that prove recent AEM hiring.
  3. Keep Naukri and LinkedIn profiles complete and current so inbound outreach can find you when the careers site is quiet.
  4. Use job boards and recruiter outreach as additional discovery channels while treating the employer's official application route as the primary source for role details.

Careers pages are useful, not complete. Evidence and hiring-evidence links in this playbook help you find where roles actually appear.

Weekly rhythm

How to search and apply on LinkedIn and Naukri after your profiles are ready.

  1. Before the last 30 days of notice: Focus on resume, profiles, company shortlists, and AEM interview prep. You may apply to MNCs and product companies that accept 60–90 day notice if your LWD matches — but avoid immediate-joiner roles if you cannot start that soon.
  2. Last 30 days of notice: On LinkedIn and Naukri, search your AEM role, set the time filter to last 30 days for a first pass, then switch to last 1 day for daily new listings. Apply only where the role fits your skills and your LWD — skip immediate-joiner posts if you could not actually start that soon.
  3. Ongoing daily search: After the backlog is cleared, search the same role with last 1 day only — apply daily to new listings while you are within your availability window.
  4. LinkedIn posts: Search AEM (or your keyword), type Posts, wider window first then last 1 day on repeat visits. Check HR hiring posts and email applications (see Outreach) when your availability matches the role's joining expectations.
  5. Platform hygiene: Use the same search strategy on LinkedIn and Naukri. Review your Naukri profile regularly and keep availability, notice period, preferred locations, skills, and experience current. Update the profile when these details change or when you improve the way your experience is presented.
  6. Stay patient: Response rates can vary throughout a notice period. If early responses are slow, continue preparing and keep your availability clear as your joining date gets closer.

Prepare early and adjust application intensity as your availability changes. Roles seeking earlier joiners may become more relevant as your last working day approaches.
