# AEM Playbook --- Editorial Tone and Messaging Update

## Objective

Review the public-facing copy in the AEM Playbook and improve its
editorial tone.

This is not a UI redesign, architecture change, or content expansion
task.

Do not change layout, CSS, navigation, component structure, JavaScript
behaviour, company data structure, filters, search, pagination, or
visual styling. Only update public-facing text where required.

## Editorial positioning

The AEM Playbook is an independent, practical career and learning
resource for AEM engineers, with a strong focus on the Indian job
market.

The content should sound practical, experienced, concise, technically
credible, transparent about uncertainty, and useful without sounding
authoritative about recruiter algorithms or hiring outcomes.

It should not sound corporate, AI-generated, motivational, like a
recruitment consultancy, like universal career rules, like undocumented
LinkedIn/Naukri algorithm claims, or like guaranteed hiring advice.

## Core editorial rule

Separate verifiable facts, practical recommendations, and uncertain
hiring or platform behaviour.

State verifiable facts directly when supported by evidence.

Present playbook strategies as recommendations rather than universal
rules. Suitable wording includes: "A practical approach is...",
"Consider...", "It can be useful to...", "For candidates serving a long
notice period...", "One way to handle this is...", and "In practice, you
may find...".

Clearly qualify uncertain hiring or platform behaviour. Avoid presenting
undocumented behaviour from LinkedIn, Naukri, ATS tools, recruiters, or
employers as fact.

Do not state "Activity improves visibility to recruiters" unless
supported by an official source. Prefer: "Keep your profile current so
recruiters see accurate availability, skills, and experience."

## Company note tone review

Review all Notes fields in company detail records.

Avoid subjective employer-review wording such as "great company", "best
employer", "strong employer", "weak employer", "easy interview", "hard
interview", "good salary", "poor culture", "stable hiring", "hiring
comes and goes", and "better than" unless directly supported by a
clearly cited source and necessary to the record.

Company notes should answer:

> Why is this company in the AEM employer database?

They should not answer:

> Is this a good company to work for?

For example, prefer:

> Adobe case study documents Cisco.com's replatforming to AEM as a Cloud
> Service. AEM-specific openings should be confirmed through current
> careers and job-board listings.

Do not turn the playbook into a company ranking or review site.

## Tone consistency rules

Use these principles only where semantically appropriate:

-   "Do not apply" → "Prioritise" or "Consider skipping"
-   "This works well" → "This provides another route"
-   "This will" → "This can"
-   "Most companies" → "Some employers"
-   Broad "Recruiters do X" claims → qualify by context
-   "Activity improves visibility" → keep information current
-   Algorithm claims → recommend a focused search routine
-   "Good enough" → use as a sanity check
-   "Target 70+" → review parsing and keyword gaps
-   "Can hurt you later" → may not be the best use of application effort
-   "Most serious offers" → roles seeking earlier joiners
-   "Agencies chase harder" → follow the stated hiring channel
-   "Higher signal" → targeted outreach
-   "Always" → usually or when applicable
-   "Never" → avoid or unless
-   "Proven" → supported by linked evidence
-   "Proof they hire AEM" → AEM hiring evidence

Do not mechanically replace every "do not", "always", "never", "will",
or "can".

Technical and safety statements can remain direct. "Do not invent resume
bullets" can remain direct.

The softening rules apply primarily to recruiter behaviour, hiring
outcomes, job-search strategy, ATS scores, LinkedIn behaviour, Naukri
behaviour, employer flexibility, and notice-period outcomes.
