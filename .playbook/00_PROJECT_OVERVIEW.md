# Project Overview

## What this is

The Enterprise Digital Experience Engineering Playbook is a long-term knowledge platform for engineers who build
and operate enterprise digital experience systems — Adobe Experience Manager (AEM), enterprise CMS/DXP platforms,
Edge Delivery Services, and the surrounding career path. It is not a one-off resume or notes file: it is designed
to be maintained, expanded, and eventually published for years.

## Who it is for

- **Primary audience:** the project owner, as a personal career operating system — tracking target companies,
  skills, interview preparation, and learning roadmaps.
- **Secondary audience (future):** the broader Enterprise Digital Experience Engineering community, once the
  content is verified and generalized enough to publish as a public reference.

## What it becomes

Per `MASTER_BOOTSTRAP_PROMPT.md`, this repository is intended to ship as:

- A GitHub repository (source of truth).
- A GitHub Pages website (public reading experience).
- A printable handbook and PDF book (offline/portable reference).
- A public knowledge base once content quality and verification bar is met.

## Scope

**In scope:** AEM and enterprise CMS/DXP engineering, Edge Delivery Services, enterprise web engineering practices,
career strategy and branding for this niche, company intelligence (who hires for these skills, and how they hire),
and the learning roadmap needed to reach and stay at a senior level in this field.

**Out of scope (for now):** general software engineering career advice not specific to digital experience
engineering, and any content that cannot be evidenced or sourced (see `07_RESEARCH_GUIDE.md`).

## Guiding priorities

In this exact order — never sacrifice a higher priority for a lower one:

1. Maintainability
2. Scalability
3. Documentation quality
4. Information architecture
5. Developer experience
6. Reader experience
7. Performance
8. Features

## How this document relates to the rest of `.playbook/`

This file answers "what and why." `01_AI_CONSTITUTION.md` answers "how AI sessions must behave."
`03_ARCHITECTURE.md` answers "how the system is built." `14_ROADMAP.md` answers "what happens next, in what order."
If any of these ever conflict, `MASTER_BOOTSTRAP_PROMPT.md` is the authoritative tie-breaker.
